Our Story

Built by hand, one cake at a time.

Maliki Bakers is the work of one woman's kitchen, grown into two.

The Founder

Meet Maximiller

Maximiller Kiganga is the baker behind Maliki Bakers — a wife, a mother of three, and someone who has spent over five years turning flour, sugar and butter into cakes people actually remember. She started the way most real bakeries start: baking for people she knew, getting asked for more, and saying yes enough times that it became a business.

Today that same hands-on approach runs through both kitchens — Mombasa and Nairobi — with Maximiller still closely involved in what goes out the door.

At a glance

Owner & Baker: Maximiller Kiganga

Experience: 5+ years in the cake industry

What Matters to Us

Our approach to baking

Fresh, Not Frozen

Baking happens every morning at both branches, in time for the day's customers — nothing is held over to the next day.

Made By Hand

Every cake that leaves Maliki Bakers is made by hand, not mass-produced — from the sponge to the icing.

Family Run

Maximiller Kiganga runs Maliki Bakers day to day — balancing the bakery with life as a wife and mother of three, and bringing over 5 years of hands-on cake-making experience to every order.

Growing, One City At A Time

From one kitchen to two

🏝️ Mombasa

The Mombasa branch leans into coastal flavour — coconut, cardamom, and the spices the region is known for.

🏙️ Nairobi

The Nairobi branch bakes the same recipes for the highlands, serving the city's everyday orders and celebration cakes alike.
